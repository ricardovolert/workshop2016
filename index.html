Learn yt Workshop 2016

 

The second yt user's workshop will be held at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications, Octover 10-12 2016. The workshop will cover basic usage of yt, including the yt data model, yt's field system, low-level data inspection, basic visualization workflows, and data analysis and reduction tasks. We will also cover more advanced usages like generating simulated observations, halo finding and halo analysis, volume rendering, advanced 3D visualizations, and advanced data analysis and reduction tasks. Finally, we will cover how to modify and extend yt, as well as the development and contribution process.

The Learn yt Workshop is supported by a National Science Foundation grant.

General Info

Organizering Committee

The main organizing committee consists of Matt Turk, Nathan Goldbaum, Jill Naiman, John ZuHone, and Kandace Turner.

The organizers can be contacted by e-mailing workshop2016@yt-project.org.

Getting to the Workshop

The workshop will be held at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA) building at the University of Illinois, Urbana Champaign. The NCSA building is on the north end of campus, about a block a way from the conference hotel.

Regional Airports

American Airlines offers regular service to the Champaign airport (CMI) from Chicago O'Hare and Dallas-Fort Worth. CMI is about 15 minutes by car away from campus. The Bloomington airport (BMI), is also relatively close, about an hour a away by car.

Chicago airports

The Champaign-Urbana area is about 2.5 hours away from the main Chicago airports by car. If you rent a car, parking is available at the conference hotel. There is also regular bus service and train service via Amtrack from Chicago Union Station to the Champaign train station.
